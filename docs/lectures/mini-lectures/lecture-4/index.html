Memory Systems

üó£Ô∏è Traditional Community Memory

  • Selective: Some things forgotten
  • Contextual: Stories change with perspective
  • Forgiving: Time heals social wounds
  • Human: Gossip, oral tradition
  • Flexible: Redemption possible

üíæ Digital Collective Memory

  • Total: Everything recorded
  • Fixed: Data points don't change
  • Unforgiving: Permanent digital record
  • Automated: Algorithms, databases
  • Rigid: Past defines future access

Key Shift: From selective human memory to comprehensive digital memory

Historical Echoes

From Bellevue Almshouse to Big Data

As historian Anelise Shrout notes about the 1840s Bellevue Almshouse records:

"This data was produced with the express purpose of reducing people to bodies; bodies to easily quantifiable aspects; and assigning value to those aspects which proved that the marginalized people to whom they belonged were worth less than their elite counterparts."

Today's Digital Equivalent:

# Same institutional power to categorize

The continuity: Technology changes, but the power to classify and exclude remains.

The Scale Problem

When Individual Decisions Become Mass Processing

üìä Traditional Scale

  • Village elder knows ~150 people
  • Decisions take time and thought
  • Mistakes affect individuals
  • Community can intervene
  • Human accountability

üåê Digital Scale

  • Algorithm processes millions instantly
  • Decisions automated and instantaneous
  • Mistakes affect entire populations
  • No human oversight at scale
  • Algorithmic accountability gap
